How can we improve on the Gen Z Employment Crisis

What Gen Z Employees Can Do to Improve Their Job Prospects

Build Real Experience Early:
Start with internships, volunteering, freelancing, or part-time jobs—even unpaid ones at first. Any experience shows effort and builds skills.

Learn In-Demand Skills:
Focus on skills employers are looking for like communication, teamwork, critical thinking, and tech tools (Excel, Canva, AI, coding, etc.). Free platforms like Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, and YouTube can help.

Work on Soft Skills:
Practice showing up on time, solving problems, and speaking professionally.

Network More:
Talk to people in industries you like. Go to events, message professionals online, or ask for advice. Connections often lead to job chances.

Create a Strong CV & Cover Letter:
Make sure your CV is clear, updated, and focused on your strengths. Use a short, personalized cover letter for each job to show you care.
